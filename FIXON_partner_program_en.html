Model

What the partner really sells

FIXON is not a heavy stock project. The partner extends the material sale with tools that make the work of paving and construction crews easier. The need appears naturally while the customer is buying kerbs, slabs, paving, sub-base or building materials.

The main benefit is not only a stronger basket. A satisfied, less tired contractor comes back sooner for the next purchase.

Core principle

Tools are attached to material delivery and real job-site needs. The sales team does not need to memorise the entire assortment because the e-catalogue guides the selection.

Start model

Simple start without a heavy rollout

Conversation and channel fit

We check whether the partner is a manufacturer, building-material wholesaler, distribution network, point of sale or tool-rental channel.

Pilot without mandatory investment

The start can be based on demonstration, QR, the e-catalogue and a narrow pilot set. A full stock position is not a requirement.

Identify needs during material purchase

During the material purchase it is easiest to ask naturally about work method, load, material type and crew size. The e-catalogue then helps select the right target tool.

Settlement after a confirmed period

Sales are not settled immediately at launch. The logic is monthly: after a confirmed sales period and report.

Sales technology

The e-catalogue removes the weight from sales

Fast tool selection

The salesperson does not need to know the whole assortment. They use application, material and customer problem as filters.

Demonstration builds trust

A short demonstration makes the tool credible and helps the customer decide without a long technical lecture.

More return visits, not just basket value

When the work gets easier, fatigue drops. The customer remembers the partner who solved that problem.

Who it is for

Channels where the model works

Material manufacturers

FIXON tools are attached to material deliveries. They are not a communication gadget, but a practical element of delivery and job-site quality.

Distribution networks and points of sale

A simple sales process that does not require a heavy implementation: QR, demo, guided selection and monthly data are enough to start.

Building-material yards and wholesalers

The need is already present in the conversation about material, delivery, crew and job site.

Tool rental

Demonstration and test logic can create demand before the customer buys.

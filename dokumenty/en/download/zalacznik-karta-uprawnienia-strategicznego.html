5. Conditions of use

The Partner shall enjoy the privileges only in the scope, time, channel, territory, product group or customer group indicated in this Charter.

Granting of power does not restrict rights Suppliers for direct customer service, complaint handling, servicing, technical communication and taking the necessary measures to protect FIXON, unless this Charter expressly states otherwise.

The power may not be transferred to another entity, establishment, related company, distributor, subcontractor or third party without consent Supplier expressed in documentary form.

The Partner may not use powers to act contrary to the Partnership Programme, in particular to price dumping, to block the market without real sales, to mislead customers or to present the scope of the mandate more broadly than is apparent from this Charter.

7. Review, restriction and revocation of the right

The supplier may review the entitlement in particular in the event of a permanent decline in sales activity, lack of reporting, infringement of the Brand Policy, infringement of the RRP rules, significant stock gap, late payment, misuse of the right or loss of the grounds for which the right was granted.

Limitation, suspension or withdrawal of the power shall require the communication of information to the Partner in documentary form, unless otherwise agreed by the Parties. The withdrawal of one strategic power shall not automatically result in the termination of the Partnership Agreement or the loss of the status of a Partner, unless circumstances require further measures provided for in the Partnership Programme.

8. Reservations and approvals

The Partner may object to this Charter within 7 days of its delivery. The absence of reservations within this deadline means the adoption of the Charter for information and application under the FIXON Partnership Programme. In the event of a statement of objections, this Charter shall not be adopted in the absence of reservations regarding the scope of the notification, and the final agreed version of the Charter shall be approved by both Parties in the form of personal signatures or electronic signatures accepted by the Parties, unless otherwise agreed by the Parties in document correspondence.

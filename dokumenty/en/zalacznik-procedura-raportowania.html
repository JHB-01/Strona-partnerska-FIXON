Objective of the procedure

The procedure lays down the rules for reporting sales, stock, products transferred to the Partner, products sold, returned, advertised, damaged, lost, transferred to the exposure, used as test tools and products available on order. The procedure is used to properly settle sales, establish discounts, update the allocated stock, operate alarm thresholds and check the compliance of cooperation with the FIXON Partner Programme.

§ 2. Persons responsible for reporting

The partner shall indicate the person responsible for the preparation and transmission of the reports and the replacement. The data of these persons should be entered in the Partner Charter or given to the Mediator in documentary form. The change of the person responsible for reporting shall require the update to be transmitted no later than 3 working days after the change.

§ 3. Deadline and form of the monthly report

The monthly report shall be submitted to the 5th day of the month following the end of the Settlement Period. The report may be sent by e-mail, by CRM system, B2B system, a form indicated by the Supplier, a report sheet or other document form accepted by the Supplier or Mediator. If the 5th day of the month falls on a public holiday, the report may be transmitted on the next working day.

§ 5. Alert threshold notification

A partner may report an alert threshold at any time if the condition of at least one item covered by storage support reaches the alert threshold specified for that item. An alert does not replace the monthly report and should cover the current state of play of all products covered by storage support in order to enable an assessment of the appropriateness of supplementing the entire warehouse to the levels allocated.

§ 6. Verification of the report

The supplier or intermediary may verify the report, request explanations, photographs of the stocks, sales records, copies of billing documents or other information necessary to determine the correct result of the sale, discount, allocated stock, alert threshold or product liability. The Partner should provide explanations within 3 working days, unless otherwise agreed by the Parties.

§ 7. No report or incomplete report

Failure to submit the report within the time limit, transmission of the incomplete report or the lack of explanation of significant discrepancies may suspend the calculation of the discount, the issue of billing documents, the addition of the warehouse, the update of the allocated stock and the granting or maintenance of additional allowances provided for in the FIXON Partnership Programme. If the Partner fails to submit the report despite the call, The supplier may settle the cooperation on the basis of available data, request inventory, suspend deliveries or apply other measures provided for in the FIXON Partnership Programme.

§ 8. Model report

The report shall be submitted in accordance with the reporting pattern indicated by the Supplier or Mediator. The model report may take the form of a spreadsheet, electronic form, PDF file, B2B system exports or other document form. The amendment of the template of the report does not require an annex if it is submitted to the Partner under the FIXON Partnership Programme in accordance with the rules for the transmission of programming documents.

§ 9. Objections clause

The Partner may object to this Reporting Procedure within 7 days of delivery. The absence of reservations within this deadline means the adoption of a procedure for informing and applying in the framework of current cooperation in the FIXON Partnership Programme. In the event of a statement of objections, the Parties shall clarify the content of the document, remove the discrepancies or prepare the final agreed version of the document. The final version of the document to which reservations have been made shall be approved by both Parties in the form of personal signatures or electronic signatures accepted by the Parties, unless otherwise agreed by the Parties in document correspondence.

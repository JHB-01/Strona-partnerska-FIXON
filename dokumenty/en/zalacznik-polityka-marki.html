ANNEX No 13 MARK AND FIXON SALE POLICY

The document sets out rules for the use of FIXON brand, marketing materials, product descriptions, sales communications, commercial arguments, advertisements, online publications and customer contact within the FIXON Partner Programme. The document may be transmitted to the Partner in documentary, electronic or as part of a package of programme annexes.

§ 1. Purpose of the document and scope

1. This FIXON Brand and Sales Communication Policy sets out the principles that the Partner should follow when presenting, promoting and selling FIXON products, whether communication takes place in a commercial facility, in the field, by telephone, by email, through a commercial representative, in a catalogue, e-catalogue, B2B system, social media, paid advertising, on a website, during fairs, shows, demonstrations or in direct conversation with the contractor.

2. The purpose of the document is to protect FIXON brand value, to preserve the coherence of the transmission, to reduce the risk of false sales promises, to support partner traders in effective argumentation and to ensure that FIXON products are presented as professional tools for the tabloid and construction industry, rather than as a random catalogue addition.

3. The policy includes in particular the use of FIXON names and designations, product description, benefit communication, price and discount communication, comparisons with competition, partner's own materials, content published on the Internet, customer answers, contact with contractors and communication in complaints, disputes or crises.

§ 2. Basic positioning of the FIXON brand

1. FIXON brand should be communicated as a Polish brand of professional tools for the paving and construction industry, focused on real improvement of the work of contractors, relief of the burden of physical workers, improvement of precision and limitation of the makeshift solutions used on construction.

2. The basic direction of sales communication should emphasize the practical value of tools: less unnecessary bending, easier grasping and moving of components, faster repetitive work, more control over positioning and profiling, less wear on improvised solutions and more order in the implementation process.

3. In communication, the presentation of FIXON products as gadgets, side accessories or exclusively promotional products should be avoided. Products should be presented as tools to support the daily work of the team, especially where repetitive small activities take time, burden workers and influence the pace of execution of the order.

4. It is acceptable to emphasise that many FIXON products either complement the market gap or replace makeshift solutions, provided that the communication does not suggest non-existent research, certificates, guaranteed results or an automatic increase in customer profit.

§ 3. Main sales communications allowed
Zone Allowed direction of communication Example of safe wording
Unloading Highlighting lower load with repeatable operations. The tool helps reduce some unnecessary movements and facilitates repetitive work on the ankle or building elements.
Operating speed Communication of improvements to work organisation, without guaranteeing a specific percentage. In many works, it allows you to perform repetitive tasks faster and better maintain the rhythm of the team's work.
Precision Highlighting control, repeatability and convenience settings. The tool helps to work more accurately and reduce improvisation at the profiling or setting stage.
Professionalisation Showing the advantage of the tool over construction solutions. Instead of makeshift solutions, the contractor gets a ready-made tool designed for a specific task.
Quality of performance Information on material and robustness if compatible with the product card. The product is made of stainless steel and designed for professional use.
§ 4. Prohibited or sensitive communications

1. The partner should not use wording that could mislead the customer about the characteristics of the product, effects of use, guaranteed working time, savings, safety, resilience, certification, exclusivity, origin or commercial conditions.

2. It is prohibited to make promises of the type: "guarantees twice faster work", "eliminates fatigue", "cannot be damaged", "best product on the market", "only such product", "always reduces labour costs" if the Partner does not have the material approved by the Supplier to confirm such a communication.

3. The Partner should not declare the technical parameters, load capacity, resistance, purpose, compatibility, warranty rules or use on its own, other than from the Supplier's product card, instructions, price list, catalogue or written information.

4. In communication, a tone offensive to contractors, competitors, other partners, material manufacturers, wholesalers or distributors should be avoided. FIXON can be communicated dynamically and specifically, but not by discrediting the market or aggressively attacking other entities.

Do not use Better version
This product will make the team work twice as fast. The product can improve some repetitive activities and help the team maintain a better pace of work.
This tool completely eliminates fatigue. The tool helps to reduce some of the loading moves and improves the comfort of operation with repetitive operations.
The competition has nothing like that. The product responds to a practical need that performers often solve on a provisional basis.
The cheapest and best on the market. A very favourable ratio of performance quality, durability and catalogue price.
Buy it now, because it won't be available later. The availability of products depends on the current states and deadlines indicated in the programming documents.
§ 5. Language of trade conversation

1. Sales communication should start with the problem of the customer, not with the list of products. The trader should first determine with which activities the customer has the biggest problem: grasping and moving elements, repetitive bending, leveling, profiling, position organization, work in larger formats, handling several people on the team, or the need to show the tool before buying.

2. The basic structure of the conversation recommended by FIXON: a diagnosis of the customer's work, an indication of the place of loss of time or load, showing the tool as a solution of a specific task, explaining how to use it, presenting the price and availability, proposing a purchase or test, and with larger orders addressed to the responsible person or Demonstration Centre.

3. A trader should not talk only in the catalogue language. FIXON products are best sold by reference to the real construction situation: repetitive lifting, improvement, measurement, transfer, profiling and organization of work around the material.

4. A simple, energetic and practical language is acceptable in conversation with the contractor, but without vulgarity, pressure, misinformation and promises not approved by the Supplier. Communication should sound like talking to a professional, not selling a random promotional product.

Conversation phase Objective Example formula
Opening Entering through a work problem. I can see that with this job the most time goes to repetitive lifting and setting of elements. We have a tool that's just this stage in order.
Diagnosis Determine whether the product fits the customer's work. Do you work more often on a small ankle, larger plates or elements that need to be set precisely?
Arguments Linking the product to the practical effect. It's not about the fun, it's about getting less tired of repeating and getting back to the right job.
Availability A clear indication of the implementation. This product is available in stock/on order according to current status in FIXON documents.
Close Making a decision. We can pick one piece at a time, or we can prepare a set for the whole team.
§ 6. Communication of prices, discounts and RRP

1. The partner should communicate the price of FIXON products in accordance with the current Catalogue Pricelist, software documents and RRP rules. The price may not be presented in a way suggesting a discount, promotion or availability which has not been confirmed in the existing documents.

2. In price communication, aggressive dumping, lowering the brand value and presenting FIXON products as a cheap additive to basic material should be avoided. It is acceptable to combine products with the Partner material offer, but the message should indicate the real value of the tool for the contractor.

3. If a Partner communicates discount, promotion, package, set or individual offer, he should ensure that the client understands what the reduction relates to, whether it includes delivery costs, what is the availability date and whether the offer is limited in time or quantity.

4. The Partner should not publicly communicate individual discount conditions, additional strategic discounts, exclusive conditions, market protection or other benefits of the Programme unless this is directly due to materials approved by the Supplier.

§ 7. Communications of accessibility and time limits

1. Information on availability of products should be based on current product status, price list, start-up warehouse list, storage support list or information received from the Supplier or Mediator. A partner should not promise the availability of “off-site” if the product is available on order or requires confirmation of the deadline.

2. The permissible communication status should correspond to the status used in the programming documents, in particular: available in the starting warehouse, subject to storage support, available immediately with delivery from the manufacturer, available on order with a deadline of 7 days, available on order with a period of up to 14 days, available as a test tool, available for purchase for a 50% discount exposure or an in-service product/out of the sales result.

3. If the deadline may be changed for production, logistics, storage or transport reasons, The partner should communicate it as an expected term or requiring confirmation rather than as a guaranteed term.

§ 8. Marketing materials and own materials of the Partner

1. The Partner may use the marketing materials provided by the Supplier or Mediator solely for the purpose of promoting and selling FIXON products as intended, without erasure of indications, distortion of content, change of technical parameters or addition of unapproved declarations.

2. The Partner may create his own advertisements, graphics, photos, films, descriptions, presentations, posts, mailings, landing page, catalogues or training materials for FIXON products, provided that these materials do not violate this Policy and will be accepted by the Supplier if they contain new product messages, comparisons, effects promises, sales passwords or intensive use of FIXON logos and markings.

3. Once accepted, the material can be reused by the Partner in the same or essentially same context, unless its meaning, content, product graphics, pricing conditions, availability status or audience change.

4. The Partner may not create materials that give the impression of the manufacturer's official website, the official FIXON account, an official certificate, an authorization higher than actually granted or the exclusivity that the Partner does not have.

§ 9. Websites, marketplace, social media and paid advertising

1. In online product descriptions FIXON Partner should use current product names, photos, prices, statuses and descriptions consistent with software documents. The description shall clearly indicate whether the product is available in stock, on order, as a test, display or other mode provided for in the Programme.

2. The partner may publish educational, guidebook, demonstration and sales content on FIXON products, including tools videos, exposure photos, presentations for tabloid teams and posts showing practical application of products, provided that they do not violate the principles of safety, dignity of employees, brand reputation or third party rights.

3. Paid advertising should not suggest that the Partner is a FIXON product manufacturer unless the message clearly indicates its actual role as a Partner, distributor, seller, demonstration centre or other entity within the Programme.

4. In advertisements, marketplace descriptions and posts, the Partner should not use clickbaits based on fear, false inaccessibility, time pressure, unfair comparison or discrediting of competition. Dynamic, practical and sales communication is acceptable, but based on real product characteristics and contractor needs.

§ 10. Comparison with competition and communication of advantages

1. Comparing FIXON products with competitive products requires special care. A partner may communicate advantages such as performance material, precision, convenience, availability, catalogue price, practicality or purpose, if they are truthful, demonstrable and do not violate the good name of competitors.

2. Public comparisons with named competitors, competition products, specific prices, technical parameters or ‘better than’ slogans require prior acceptance of the Supplier unless the Partner uses a comparative material prepared or approved by the Supplier.

3. A safer form of communication advantage is showing the construction problem and how it is solved by FIXON, without indicating a specific competitor. Recommended communication: "Instead of extrapolating a makeshift solution on construction, you can use a tool designed for this operation".

§ 11. Communication of complaints, phrases and disputed situations

1. In case of complaint, return, delay, warehouse discrepancy or customer dissatisfaction, the Partner should communicate calmly, factually and in accordance with FIXON procedures. The customer should not be promised the recognition of the complaint, exchange, discount, refund or delivery date if the matter requires a decision of the Supplier or Mediator.

2. The partner should avoid passing the blame on the client, crew, producer, carrier or other party without prior verification. The acceptable message should read: "We accept the notification, we will transmit it for verification and return with a response in accordance with the procedure".

3. In public transport, in particular in Internet and social media comments, The Partner should not disclose client data, details of the dispute, commercial documents, individual terms or internal arrangements of the program.

§ 12. Training of the Partner team and responsibility for communication

1. The Partner should ensure that those involved in sales, customer service, marketing, exposure, e-commerce and reporting know the basic principles of this Policy and use up-to-date material and programming documents.

2. The supplier or intermediary may provide the Partner with additional training materials, conversation scripts, argument cards, answers to the most common questions, post patterns, product descriptions, photos, videos and recommendations for use. These materials should be treated as a preferred communication pattern.

3. The partner shall be responsible for the communication carried out by his institutions, representatives, employees, subcontractors, marketing agencies and persons acting on his behalf in the promotion or sale of FIXON products.

§ 13. Control, correction and infringement of the Policy

1. The Supplier or Mediator may request the Partner to change, delete or correct the message, advertising, description, graphics, film, post, offers, website, campaign or material if it violates this Policy, Software Documents, RRP Rules, Brand Rights or FIXON Interest.

2. The Partner should make the required adjustment without delay, no later than the time specified by the Supplier or the Mediator, and if the matter concerns a communication that may mislead customers, infringe the law, suggest non-existent terms or harm the brand, the correction should be carried out without undue delay.

3. Repeated violations of the Policy may justify limiting marketing materials, limiting programming rights, suspending consent to campaigns, withdrawing consent to the use of selected materials, changing the status of a Partner or other activities provided for in the Partnership Programme and cooperation documents.

§ 15. Objections clause and approval

The document submitted to the Partner under the FIXON Partnership Programme does not require a personal signature unless the Partner objects to its content within 7 days of its delivery. The absence of reservations within that time limit means the acceptance of a document for information and use in the framework of ongoing cooperation. The document may also bear an electronic signature, a digital signature, a trusted signature, a qualified electronic signature or another form of electronic confirmation accepted by the Parties. If the Partner raises objections to the document within 7 days of its delivery, the document shall not be adopted in the absence of reservations in the scope of the notification and the final agreed version shall be approved by both Parties in the form of personal signatures or electronic signatures accepted by the Parties, unless otherwise agreed by the Parties in document correspondence.

CHAPTER 1
NATURE, OBJECTIVE AND MODEL OF THE FIXON PARTNERSHIP PROGRAMME

1.1. Nature of the FIXON Partnership Programme

The FIXON Partnership Programme is a document setting out the principles of participation of Partners in the system of commercial, distribution, exhibition and warehouse cooperation organized by FIXON.

The FIXON Partnership Programme is an annex to the FIXON Partnership Agreement and an implementing document specifying the principles of cooperation insofar as the Agreement refers to the Partnership Programme, Program Documents, current product lists, price lists, exposure standards, storage support rules, discount system, strategic powers and status of the FIXON Demonstration Centre.

The FIXON Partner Programme is a document made available to the Partners for inspection and consultation, in particular through publication on the FIXON website or transmission in documentary form.

Public provision of the Programme aims to ensure transparency of cooperation rules, to enable potential partners to familiarise themselves with the FIXON distribution model and to facilitate the uniform application of the Programme rules to participants in the partner network.

The FIXON Partnership Programme lays down general principles of cooperation and does not replace individual trade arrangements concluded with the Partner.

Individual terms of cooperation, in particular the discounts granted, the range of stocks, the list of products covered by storage support, the scope of the exposure, the awarded Strategic Powers, the status of the Strategic Partner, the status of the FIXON Demonstration Centre and the special commercial conditions, are set out in the FIXON Partnership Agreement or separate documents, in particular in the Partner Charter, the Strategic Powers Charter, the FIXON Demonstration Centre Charter, the List of Products Covered by Storage Support, the Catalogue Price List or other Program Documents.

Signing the FIXON Partnership Agreement means accepting the current version of the FIXON Partnership Programme, unless the Parties expressly agree otherwise.

The FIXON Partnership Programme should be interpreted together with the FIXON Partnership Agreement and other Program Documents.

In the event of a discrepancy between the FIXON Partnership Programme and the FIXON Partnership Agreement, priority shall be given to the provisions of the FIXON Partnership Agreement unless the Parties expressly agree otherwise.

In the event of a discrepancy between the FIXON Partnership Programme and the individual document granting the Partner certain terms of cooperation, priority shall be given to the individual conditions confirmed in such a document, unless they infringe the provisions of the FIXON Partnership Agreement.

1.2. Objective of the FIXON Partnership Programme

The FIXON Partner Programme aims to build a professional, stable and long-term distribution network for FIXON products.

The program was created to allow Partners to expand their offer with specialist FIXON tools without the need to create a full, costly and distributed warehouse.

The programme is intended to enable Partners to sell FIXON products in a manner adapted to their existing business model, in particular by:

  • sales of products in the partner's stock,
  • sales of products available from the catalogue,
  • sales of delivered products directly from the Supplier,
  • sale of products to individual customers,
  • the sale of products together with building, paving, road or other products offered by the Partner,
  • presentation of products through physical exposure, marketing materials, test tools, e-catalogue and demonstration materials.

The program is intended to provide the final customer with easier access to professional tools, and the Partner can increase sales value, expand offer and build competitive advantage.

The FIXON Partnership Programme shall in particular pursue the following objectives:

  • increasing the availability of FIXON products on the market,
  • creation of a controlled distribution network,
  • enabling Partners to sell tools without having to fully stock each facility,
  • optimising logistics costs,
  • support the sale of basic materials offered by the Partner,
  • improving the quality of work with materials sold by the Partner,
  • shortening the working time of executive teams by using appropriate tools,
  • increase the attractiveness of the Partner's offer in the eyes of contractors and end customers,
  • the development of FIXON products based on market feedback,
  • FIXON value protection,
  • building long-term commercial relations with Partners.

The program assumes that professional tools are not only an additional sales product, but an element supporting the entire process of construction, paving, road and related work.

Sales of FIXON tools can benefit the Partner not only through margin on the tools themselves, but also by increasing the value of the basic offer. In the case of manufacturers or distributors of materials, the use of appropriate tools may improve the quality of the work with these materials, reduce the number of executive errors, reduce crew working time and increase the satisfaction of end customers.

The FIXON Partnership Programme is intended to support Partners who have real access to contractors, tabloid companies, construction companies, road companies, wholesale customers, investors and other customer groups that can benefit from FIXON products.

1.3. FIXON cooperation model

The FIXON Partner Programme is based on a distribution model combining sales, product exposure, warehouse support, catalogue sales, direct deliveries and partner development within a specific cooperation hierarchy.

The Program model assumes that the Partner does not need to maintain the full stock of all FIXON products.

The partner can start cooperation with the agreed starting warehouse, marketing materials, demonstration tools and access to FIXON e-catalogue products.

Products not included in the starting warehouse may be offered to customers from the catalogue, including as products available immediately with delivery from the Supplier, products available on order or products available on individual terms.

With the development of sales, the Partner can gain increasing access to warehouse stocks, additional demonstration tools, special products, commercial benefits and Strategic Rights.

The programme provides for three levels of cooperation:

  • Authorised Partner FIXON,
  • Strategic Partner FIXON,
  • FIXON Demonstration Center.

Each level of cooperation corresponds to a different scope of involvement of the Partner and a different level of benefit.

Authorised Partner FIXON receives basic tools to launch sales, in particular marketing materials, access to offers, the possibility of using the Warehouse States Support Program and the possibility of using demonstration tools.

The Strategic Partner FIXON may receive specific Strategic Entitlement if its sales model, customer base, market activity, need to protect the sales channel, above average results, sell products on order or participate in product development justify granting additional terms of cooperation.

The FIXON Demonstration Centre is the highest level of cooperation and can serve as a showroom, a demonstration facility, a regional test point, a direct sales point, a special order handling post and a place to which customers are directed requiring full presentation of FIXON products.

1.4. Public nature of the Programme and individual conditions of the Partner

The FIXON Partnership Programme may be publicly available as it describes the overall model of cooperation and the principles of participation in the FIXON Partnership Network.

The public nature of the Programme does not mean that each Partner automatically has all the benefits, statuses, allowances, discounts, storage limits or special conditions described therein.

The granting of certain benefits depends on the Supplier's decision, the provisions of the Partnership Agreement, current Program Documents and individual arrangements with the Partner.

In particular, a public description of elements such as Strategic Partner, Additional Strategic Discount, Market Protection, Extended Warehouse States Support Program, Product Exclusive, Premier Exclusive or FIXON Demonstration Centre does not constitute an obligation Suppliers to grant such benefits to each Partner.

The individual conditions of the Partner must be confirmed in the relevant document, in particular:

  • The FIXON Partnership Agreement,
  • Partner card,
  • The Strategic Power Charter,
  • The FIXON Demonstration Centre card,
  • Lists of products covered by storage support,
  • Catalog Pricelist,
  • a separate agreement between the Parties,
  • another Program Document.

If a Partner benefits from a specific benefit but has not been individually confirmed, the Partner shall be deemed to benefit exclusively from the basic scope of the Programme or the Agreement, unless the Parties have expressly decided otherwise.

1.5. Principle of not worsening the conditions of the Partner

The FIXON Partnership Programme may be updated with the development of the offer, the change of the distribution model, the change of the exposure rules, the change of technical documents, the development of e-catalogues, the change of supply organisation, the introduction of new products or the change of the partner network development strategy.

The Programme updates should not impair the individual conditions of the Partners resulting from existing Partnership Agreements or individually confirmed Program Documents.

The change in the public content of the Programme does not automatically result in the loss of individually granted conditions if they are confirmed in the Agreement, the Partner Charter, the Charter of Strategic Powers or any other document in force between the Parties.

If the update of the Programme requires the individual conditions of the Partner, The Supplier shall communicate to the Partner the change as provided for in the Agreement or Program Documents.

Changes to the Programme and Program Documents shall apply from the date indicated by the Supplier, but not earlier than the next Settlement Period.

Technical changes or benefits to the Partner shall also apply from the date indicated by the Supplier, but not earlier than the subsequent Settlement Period, unless the Parties individually decide otherwise in acceptable form.

The current version of the FIXON Partnership Program can be published on the FIXON website.

Partners may be informed of changes to the Programme by the Supplier or Mediator, in particular via e-mail, communicator, CRM system, B2B system or other document form.

1.6. Principle of cooperation based on real market access

The FIXON Partnership Programme is aimed primarily at Partners with real access to end customers, contractors, tabloid companies, construction companies, road companies, installation companies, infrastructure companies, wholesale customers, investors or other professional customers.

FIXON does not build the Program based on accidental and uncontrolled sales, but on cooperation with Partners who can actually support market development, product presentation, customer feedback and implementation of new solutions.

Partner's access to the market may be due in particular to:

  • having its own sales facilities,
  • the holding of partner distribution points,
  • holding a network of commercial representatives,
  • operating executive companies,
  • wholesale customer service,
  • the operation of investments,
  • the sale of building, paving or road materials,
  • supplies to final customers,
  • organisation of demonstrations, trainings or industrial events,
  • cooperation with designers, investors or contractors.

Partner participating in the Program should use their own sales and customer contact channels to promote FIXON products in accordance with the Programme, the Agreement and brand protection rules.

A particularly important value for FIXON is the Partner's ability to provide feedback from the market. This information may concern the needs of contractors, problems arising from construction, expected product parameters, missing solutions, new tools and customer response to products already available in the offer.

A partner actively participating in the development of products whose feedback will lead to the creation or implementation of a new product may obtain specific conditions for cooperation in the field of that product, in particular Premiere Exclusive or other Strategic Entitlement as defined in the Programme.

1.7. Principle of optimization of sales and logistics

One of the basic principles of the FIXON Partner Programme is to allow Partners to sell professional tools without having to fully store the entire offer in each facility or channel of distribution.

The programme involves using several parallel forms of product availability:

  • starting warehouse,
  • Warehouse States Support Program,
  • products available immediately with delivery from the Supplier,
  • products available on request,
  • demonstration products,
  • test products,
  • Prime products,
  • products available on individual terms.

Thanks to this, the Partner can develop the sale of FIXON products in a gradual manner, proportional to the real interest of customers and without having to over-involve its own resources in stocking the entire offer.

This model is particularly beneficial when FIXON products are sold together with the basic materials offered by the Partner. In this case, the delivery of tools can be combined with the delivery of material to the final customer, which allows to reduce the unit cost of logistics.

Optimization of logistics increases the attractiveness of the Partner's offer and can facilitate the sale of both FIXON tools and basic materials offered by the Partner.

The use of appropriate tools by the contractor may have a positive impact on the pace and quality of work, which indirectly supports the reputation of the materials provided by the Partner and increases the chance for repetitive orders.

1.8. Principle of development by results, market needs and feedback

The FIXON Partnership Programme foresees the development of the Partner based on actual results, potential for cooperation, the needs of the sales channel and feedback from the market.

The development of the Partner may result from both permanent, repetitive sales results and from a one-time but significant business event that creates a strategic value for FIXON.

Such an event may in particular be:

  • acquisition of a large wholesale customer,
  • creation of a database of permanent recipients,
  • development of a significant sales channel,
  • participation in the development of a new product,
  • providing feedback leading to the implementation of the new solution,
  • justification for the need to protect a particular sales channel,
  • the purchase of custom-made products on a scale justifying the storage of such products,
  • creating the conditions for launching the FIXON Demonstration Centre.

The program does not treat the Strategic Partner solely as a prize for sales volume. Strategic status may also be due to the role of the Partner in market development, product development, customer service, optimization of the sales channel or construction of a new product category.

Granting a certain status or Strategic Powers is intended to ensure the cost-effectiveness of cooperation for both parties, rather than merely one-sided bonusping of the Partner.

The Supplier may grant the Partner such Strategic Entitlement, which best corresponds to the source of value created by the Partner.

If the Partner prefers a different Strategic Entitlement than proposed by the Supplier, the Parties may agree on a different cooperation model if it is in accordance with the FIXON interest and objectives of the Programme.

1.9. Principle of brand protection and network stability

The FIXON Partner Programme assumes the development of sales while protecting brand value, stability of commercial conditions and profitability of partner cooperation.

FIXON does not support a sales model based solely on aggressive price competition that could lead to a reduction in the perceived value of products, destabilization of the partner network or a reduction in professional customer service.

Partners participating in the Programme should promote FIXON products as professional tools supporting productivity, ergonomics and quality of work.

Partners should present products according to their actual characteristics, application, parameters and materials made available by FIXON.

Any promotional, marketing, exhibition and sales activities should be carried out in accordance with the Agreement, Program, FIXON Brand Policy and other Program Documents.

1.10. Importance of the Programme to the Partner

Participation in the FIXON Partner Programmeme allows the Partner to develop an additional product category without the need to build the entire product, marketing, exhibition and training system.

The partner is given the opportunity to sell products responding to the real needs of contractors and professional customers.

The program allows Partner:

The program was designed so that the Partner could start cooperation with a simple sales model and then develop it depending on the real potential of the market, sales results, activity of the facilities and involvement in product development.

1.11. Importance of the Programme to FIXON

FIXON Partner Programme allows FIXON to develop a controlled distribution network based on Partners with real contact with the executive market.

The program allows FIXON to increase the availability of products, conduct exposure at partner sales points, test new products, collect feedback and develop the offer based on actual customer needs.

The partners who:

  • have access to economic operators,

1.12. Basic interpretative assumption

The FIXON Partnership Programme should be interpreted in accordance with its objective of developing a professional, stable and controlled distribution network for FIXON products.

The specific provisions of the Programme shall be applied in such a way as to support:

  • development of sales,

  • FIXON brand protection,

  • long-term viability of cooperation for Partner and FIXON.

The scheme should not be interpreted in such a way as to lead to the automatic right of the Partner to obtain a certain status, discount, storage limit, exclusivity, market protection or other benefit if such benefit has not been granted to the Partner in the relevant document.

At the same time, the Programme should be applied in a transparent, coherent and predictable manner so that the Partner can understand the principles of the development of cooperation and the conditions that may lead to a higher level of partnership or additional powers.

In many channels of sale of building, paving and road materials, the main offer of the Partner includes material, while specialist tools remain an additional category, often unavailable from the moment of delivery or occasionally ordered.

In practice, this means that the customer who buys the material often has to look for tools elsewhere, order them separately, bear additional delivery costs or use random and makeshift solutions.

The FIXON Partner Programme responds to this problem by creating a model in which the Partner can offer FIXON tools without the need to maintain the full stock of the entire offer.

The partner may start selling FIXON products based on:

  • starting warehouse,
  • the underlying exposure,
  • test tools,
  • marketing materials,
  • e-catalogue,
  • products available immediately with delivery from the Supplier,
  • products available on request,
  • commercial and training support.

This enables the Partner to include FIXON products in its offer without having to bear high initial costs and without the risk of over-stocking products that have not yet been confirmed.

This model allows the Partner to check the actual interest of customers, collect feedback, develop sales gradually and expand the warehouse only if the sale of a given item justifies its constant availability.

2.4. Tool model with material

One of the leading uses of the FIXON Partner Programme is the sale of FIXON products together with materials offered by the Partner.

This applies in particular to the situation where the Partner sells to the customer building, paving, road or other basic material, while at the same time offering tools to facilitate its proper assembly, transport, laying, profiling or processing.

In such a model, FIXON tools become a natural supplement to the material order.

The benefit for the customer is the ability to purchase material and tools in one place, without the need to search for additional solutions.

The benefit for the Partner is the possibility of:

  • increase the value of the purchasing basket,
  • extend the offer without the need to build a full tool warehouse,
  • offer the customer a complete solution,
  • improving the quality of contractor service,
  • increase customer loyalty,
  • distinguishing from competition,
  • the use of existing material logistics.

If the Partner carries out the delivery of the material to the final customer, FIXON products can be delivered together with this material, provided that logistical arrangements, availability of products and the way in which the order is carried out allow.

The combination of tool supply and material supply may reduce the unit logistics cost and increase the attractiveness of the price presented to the customer.

In the case of products available with delivery from the Supplier, the price presented to the Customer may take account of the cost of delivery according to the terms specified in the Price list, e-catalogue or other Program Document.

The sales model together with the material is of particular importance in the tabloid industry, where the quality and pace of the contractor's work influence the final reception of the entire investment.

Tools for improving ergonomics, precision and organization of work can indirectly support sales of Partner's materials because the customer receives not only the basic product, but also solutions to facilitate its professional use.

2.6. Benefits for the final customer

The FIXON Partner Programme was also designed for the final customer, who expects simple access to professional tools, product advice and solutions that meet real working conditions.

The final customer can use the FIXON offer through the Partner, who already buys materials, receives orders or uses commercial advice. Thus, purchasing tools does not require the customer to seek a separate supplier, to place a separate order or to arrange an additional delivery.

For the final customer, the most important benefits of the Programme are:

  • possibility to purchase tools together with material,
  • access to professional tools for the tabloid, construction, road and executive industries,
  • the possibility to get acquainted with products through exposure, test tools, e-catalogue, demonstration materials or commercial consultation,
  • the possibility of placing an order from the catalogue also when the product is not physically located in the Partner's facility,
  • the possibility of using the supply of tools together with the basic material,
  • access to special, test, premiere or custom-made products through Strategic Partners or FIXON Demonstration Centres,
  • the ability to better select tools for the type of work done,
  • reducing the risk of buying an accidental or mismatched tool.

FIXON products should be presented to the final customer, not as an accidental addition to the sale of material, but as part of a complete implementation process. The customer who buys the cube, slabs, curbs, concrete elements or other materials needs not only the material itself, but also tools that allow for more efficient, more precise and less burdensome assembly.

In communication with the final customer, the trader should stress that FIXON products are professional tools, made for intensive work under construction conditions. The manufacturer indicates that FIXON tools are made of stainless steel in Poland, and their aim is to save time, increase precision and improve the quality of work.

One of the key benefits for the final customer is to reduce the physical effort of employees. In many tabloid and construction works, the waste of time and fatigue are not due to large operations only, but to thousands of repetitive, minor activities: bending to the ankle, improving elements, moving plates, setting edges, profiling substrates or using makeshift solutions made on construction.

FIXON products should be presented as tools to help reduce the number of such repetitive loads. For the contractor, this means a practical benefit: the worker is less tired, he returns more quickly to the next activity, and the team can maintain a better pace of work for the longer part of the day.

The trader can explain to the customer that the tool does not need to replace a large machine to actually improve the performance of the work. Often the biggest effect is given by tools that improve small but constantly repeated activities. If any such activity takes less time, requires less force or is performed more accurately, the entire team gains time during the working day.

The second important advantage is to increase the precision of work. The tools used to guide, profile, capture, carry or set the elements reduce improvisation on construction. Instead of using random boards, rods, ropes, washers or hand-made solutions, the contractor can use a tool designed for a specific operation.

Precision matters not only for the contractor, but also for the final customer and Partner selling the material. The better the work done, the less risk of complaint, amendment, inequality, material damage or investor discontent. FIXON tools can therefore indirectly support the quality of performance made using materials purchased from the Partner.

The third advantage is better organisation of work. A performer who has the appropriate tools may limit the number of interruptions, corrections and “on-the-tense” activities. For the customer, this means a more efficient implementation and for the Partner the opportunity to present the offer as more complete and professional.

In the case of tools for grasping, carrying and setting elements, the trader should emphasize in particular:

  • a stronger and more secure grip,
  • greater control of the component,
  • lower load on hands, back and knees,
  • less risk of accidental drop,
  • faster pace of operation with repetitive operations,
  • more comfortable work with heavier elements,
  • better stability during handling and setting.

For example, the product description of the GRIX HOLD gripper points to a strong, precise grip, stability when moving the ankle and heavy concrete plates, resistance to corrosion and intensive operation, and real acceleration of the working speed. Such arguments should be used by traders as a concrete answer to the customer’s question: ‘Why do I need this tool?’.

In the case of tools for profiling, running and levelling the area, the trader should emphasize in particular:

  • replacing makeshift solutions,
  • repeatability of settings,
  • greater control of the end result,
  • shortening the preparation of the workstation,
  • limitation of amendments,
  • easier to maintain an equal level,
  • greater predictability of team work.

In the case of specialist tools or system kits, the trader should stress that the customer does not buy a single gadget, but a solution to a specific implementing problem. If the product reduces working time, reduces fatigue and improves precision, its value for the contractor is not due solely to the purchase price, but to the saving of time and quality of work on subsequent orders.

The durability of products is also an important commercial argument. FIXON tools should be presented as solutions for regular, professional work rather than as disposable or seasonal equipment. Information about stainless steel and the manufacturer's emphasis on durability and quality of performance strengthen the message that the customer invests in a tool for long-term use.

The trader should avoid bringing the conversation only to the price of the tool. The right argument should focus on the total benefit for the contractor, i.e. how much time, force, amendment and improvisation can be reduced by using the appropriate tool.

For the contractor, the most important question is not only “how much does the tool cost?” but also:

  • how many times a day do I perform a given activity,
  • How much time I waste repeating it,
  • how much force it costs workers,
  • how many errors occur by improvisation,
  • how many amendments can be avoided,
  • whether the tool will allow the crew to work faster and more equally,
  • whether the tool will improve the comfort of work,
  • whether this will make me move on to the next stage of the work.

From the final customer's point of view, FIXON products can therefore fulfil several functions simultaneously:

  • sales tool — because you can buy it together with material,
  • organizational tools — because they order construction work,
  • ergonomic tools — because they reduce the load on workers,
  • qualitative tool — because it helps perform work more precisely,
  • economic tools — because they can reduce working time and the number of amendments,
  • Image tool — because a team using professional equipment looks more credible in the eyes of the investor.

The final customer does not need to know the whole structure of the FIXON Partner Programme. From its perspective, the most important thing is that the Partner can offer a solution tailored to work needs, available in a simple purchasing model, with the possibility of presentation, order or delivery together with the material.

The program is intended to make it easier for Partners to conduct sales so that the end customer understands that FIXON tools help solve specific implementation problems: fatigue of employees, waste of time, temporary adjustment of elements, difficulty in precision, need for adjustments and dissipation of purchases between many suppliers.

The most effective commercial argument should start with the problem of the customer, not the product itself. The trader should first determine whether the customer is doing the work of tabloid, construction, road, transport or assembly, and then indicate which FIXON tool can simplify the specific activity performed by his crew.

An example of the logic of a trade conversation may be as follows:

  • “Does the team often move ankles, plates or heavier pieces by hand?”
  • “Do you waste a lot of time improving settings, levels or driving?”
  • “Does it happen that on construction you make temporary extras?”
  • “Do you want to buy the material itself, or a set of solutions that will facilitate its efficient installation?”
  • “This tool is not an addition to the order — it can speed up work with material you are already buying.”

From the point of view of the Partner, it is particularly important that a well-chosen tool can increase customer satisfaction with basic material. If the contractor works faster and more accurately with the purchased material, it is better to evaluate the entire purchase process, not just the tool itself.

FIXON products should therefore be presented as part of a complete offer for the contractor: material, tool, working method and end result. Such a sales model increases the value for the final customer and at the same time strengthens the Partner's position as a supplier of practical solutions, not just the material sales point.

3.8. Character of the Strategic Partner by source of advantage

The FIXON Partner Programme assumes that the Strategic Partner may have different sources of advantage.

The market profile concerns a Partner whose channel of sale, territory, industry, customer group, project or investment justifies granting Market Protection. The warehouse profile concerns a Partner who generates fixed, above average sales results or regularly sells products available on request. In this case, expanding storage support can reduce logistics costs, shorten customer service times and increase product availability.

The Product or Premier Profile concerns a Partner whose feedback from the market has led to the creation of a new product or an important product variant. In such a case, the Partner may obtain an Exclusive Premiere or other specific conditions for the implementation of the product.

Strategic profiles are used to describe the logic of granting allowances. They are not a closed catalogue of cases where the Supplier can grant the Partner the status of Strategic Partner.

Exposures to non-financial corporations

5.1. Objective of exposure of FIXON products

The exhibition of FIXON products is one of the basic elements of the FIXON Partnership Program.

The purpose of the exposure is to allow the final customer to familiarise themselves with the FIXON brand, the basic function of the products, their use and the benefits of using professional tools in the executive work.

The exhibition does not have to rely on physical presentation of the entire FIXON product offer in each Partner facility.

The programme shall include a mixed exposure model, including in particular:

  • marketing materials,
  • FIXON brand markings,
  • QR code leading to e-catalogue or demonstration materials,
  • one test tool from the master exposure pool,
  • selected products available in the starting warehouse,
  • access to a fuller offer through a catalogue, e-catalogue or commercial consultation,
  • the ability to refer the client to the FIXON Demonstration Centre if such a Centre functions in a particular cooperation model.

The exhibition is intended to support sales but at the same time should not overburden the partner's organisational facilities.

The FIXON exhibition model is designed to enable sales even in facilities that do not have a separate tool section or a large exhibition space.

The main purpose of the exhibition is to draw the client's attention to the fact that the Partner can buy not only material, but also tools to facilitate working with this material.

The exposition should help traders to start a conversation with the customer and direct it to the right purchase path: a product available in stock, a product from a catalogue, a custom product, a test tool or a Demonstration Centre.

5.2. Exposure function on sale

The exhibition of FIXON products serves as information, sales, demonstration and image.

The information function is that the customer learns about the availability of FIXON products from the Partner.

The sales function is that the exposure increases the chance to purchase the tool together with the basic material or as a standalone product.

The demonstration function is that the customer can see or test the selected tool, and in the absence of a physical exposure of the full offer he can view demonstration materials online.

The image function is that the Partner presents himself as a provider of complete solutions for contractors, not just as a point of sale of material.

The exposition should help the client understand that FIXON tools respond to practical work problems, such as:

  • fatigue of workers,
  • repeated bending and moving of components,
  • difficulty in precise adjustment of materials,
  • makeshift construction solutions,
  • waste of time on ancillary activities,
  • the need for amendments,
  • no specialised tools available at the time of purchase of the material.

A properly prepared exposure should shorten the customer's path from interest to purchase. The customer should easily notice the FIXON brand, understand the basic use of the product and be able to move to more complete information through QR code, e-catalogue, conversation with the trader or test tool.

CHAPTER 7
MAGAZINE STATE SUPPORT PROGRAMME

7.1. Purpose of the Warehouse States Support Program

The Warehouse States Support Program is one of the basic mechanisms of the FIXON Partner Programme.

The aim of the Warehouse States Support Program is to provide Partner with the ability to conduct the current sale of selected FIXON products without the need to finance the full product warehouse independently.

The program is intended to enable the Partner to maintain warehouse stocks of those products whose sales have been confirmed by the real interest of customers.

The Warehouse States Support Program shall serve in particular:

  • increasing the availability of FIXON products with the Partner,
  • limiting customer waiting time on the most sold products,
  • optimization of logistics costs,
  • limitation of accidental handling,
  • matching warehouse level to real sales,
  • supporting the active sale of FIXON products,
  • construction of a predictable model of state replenishment,
  • reducing the storage risk on the partner's side.

The scheme is based on the assumption that fixed stock should be maintained primarily for products whose sales have reached a level justifying their availability from the outset.

The scheme is not a one-time transfer of a large warehouse, but a cyclical adjustment of stock levels to sales results and actual market demand.

7.2. Scope of the Warehouse States Support Program

The Warehouse States Support Scheme may include the products indicated by the Supplier in the Storage Support List.

The list of products covered by Warehouse Support is determined individually for each Partner.

The list shall not be determined separately for each Partner's facility unless the Parties decide otherwise individually.

If the Partner has multiple facilities, the way in which the products covered by the storage support are placed between the Facility belongs to the Partner.

The programme may include in particular:

  • products available in the starting warehouse,
  • products available in storage support,
  • products available immediately with delivery from the Supplier,
  • products available on order with delivery from the Supplier,
  • products available on an individual basis,
  • test or premiere products, if covered by the Programme,
  • exhibition or demonstration products, if so marked,
  • operating products excluded from the Sales Result if they are listed for information.

The mere placement of the product in the FIXON offer does not automatically mean that the product is covered by storage support from a given Partner.

The scope of the Programme for a Partner is based on the current Product List covered by Warehouse Support and other individual Program Documents.

7.3. Conditions for placing the product under storage support

After the first Clearance Period, the product may be subject to storage support if, in accordance with the rules of the Programme, the number of units allocated to that product is at least 5.

The number of units allocated is calculated as 40% of the highest Sales Result of a given item achieved by the Partner in one Settlement Period from up to twelve previous Settlement Periods.

The result of the calculation shall be rounded up to the full piece.

This means that, in principle, the minimum monthly Sales result of a given item, which warrants the commencement of continuous storage support for that item, is 11 units.

If the product has not reached the result justifying the granting of at least 5 warehouse support units, it may continue to be offered to customers from the catalogue, e-catalogue, delivery from the Supplier or as a custom product.

The supplier may grant storage support for a product which does not comply with the above condition only in specific cases where this is justified by the strategy of product implementation, promotion action, customer character, partner status, status of the Demonstration Centre or individual arrangements.

The granting of such support shall be individual and shall be recorded in the Product List covered by Storage Support or other Programme Document.

7.4. Principle of calculation of stock level

After the first Settlement Period, the level of stock for each Product range item is determined on the basis of the actual Sales Result.

The basic level of storage support is 40% of the highest Sales result of a given position achieved by the Partner in one Settlement Period from up to twelve previous Settlement Periods.

The calculation shall be made separately for each Productive Position.

The result of the calculation shall always be rounded up to the full piece.

Technical example:

  • if the highest Sales result of the item was 11 units, the level of support shall be 5 units,
  • if the highest Sales result of a given item was 20 units, the level of support shall be 8 units,
  • if the highest Sales result of a given item was 50 units, the level of support shall be 20 units.

These examples are explanatory and do not replace the individual List of Products covered by Storage Support.

The level of storage support may be changed in subsequent Settlement Periods if the result of sales, availability of products, Partner status or the scope of the allowances granted changes.

7.5. Best score of up to twelve Settlement Periods

The basis for establishing the stock level is the best sales result of the item from up to twelve previous Settlement Periods.

The principle of the best result is to support the development of sales and limit the situation where a one-off drop in sales immediately reduces the availability of the product with the Partner.

This mechanism allows the stock to be maintained at a level corresponding to the best proven sales, rather than just the last month.

At the same time, the Programme does not provide for an unlimited stock where demand for the product ceases to be confirmed for subsequent periods.

The supplier may analyse sales history, product rotation, seasonality, order structure and other factors affecting the reasonableness of storage.

Where sales of the product are seasonal, The supplier may take seasonality into account when updating the List of Products covered by Storage Support.

If the product ceases to rotate or if further storage is not justified, The supplier may change its status to a catalogue product, a custom product or a product available on individual terms.

7.6. Emergency threshold

For each Product range item covered by the Storage States Support Program, an Emergency Threshold is set.

The Alarm threshold is 25% of the allocated stock level, not less than 2 units.

The Alarm threshold means the level at which the Partner should report the need to replenish the stock.

The Alarm threshold is set for each position separately.

The Alert threshold should be visible in the Storage Support Product List.

Technical example:

  • if the level of storage is 8 units, the Alert threshold is 2 units,
  • if the level of storage is 20 units, the Alert threshold is 5 units,
  • if the level of storage is 40, the Alert threshold is 10.

The Emergency threshold is to provide time to replenish the products before the entire stock is exhausted.

The achievement of the Alert Threshold by one item triggers the possibility of reporting completion of the entire warehouse to the levels allocated at the beginning of the given Settlement Period.

7.7. Alert

The Partner may submit an alert at any time of the Settlement Period if at least one Product range reaches the Alarm Threshold.

An emergency notification should contain the current storage status of all items covered by the Storage States Support Program.

The requirement to indicate the status of all items results from the fact that after an emergency notification the Supplier may supplement the entire warehouse to the levels granted at the beginning of the relevant Settlement Period.

An emergency notification should enable the Supplier or the Mediator to determine:

  • which position reached the Emergency threshold,
  • what are the current states of other positions,
  • what quantity of products is needed to replenish storage,
  • whether there are differences in the state,
  • whether there are damaged, lost, advertised or withdrawn products from current sales.

An alert may be filed in a form agreed with the Supplier or Mediator, in particular by email, form, sheet, CRM system, B2B system or other document form.

An emergency notification shall not replace a monthly sales report unless the Supplier or the Mediator expressly decides otherwise.

If the emergency call is incomplete, The supplier or intermediary may call on the Partner to supplement it before completing the complementary delivery.

7.8. Stockfill

After receiving a proper emergency call The supplier can complete the partner's stock.

The supplement may include the entire warehouse covered by the Warehouse Support Scheme and not just the product that reached the Emergency Threshold.

The objective of this model is to reduce the number of partial deliveries and optimise logistics costs.

The supplement shall be made in principle to the stock levels granted at the beginning of the relevant Settlement Period.

Complementary deliveries may be made no more than once over seven consecutive calendar days.

The reduction in the frequency of deliveries is aimed at maintaining a rational logistics model and avoiding excessive fragmentation of supplies.

If, within the same seven-day period, the next position reaches the Alert Threshold, The supplier may decide to supplement it with another intended delivery unless the Parties agree otherwise.

The supplier may make the delivery of the complementary supply subject to proper sales reporting, lack of late payment and the absence of significant stock differences.

CHAPTER 8
DISCOUNT SYSTEM AND RESULT OF SALE

8.1. Purpose of the discount scheme

The FIXON Discount System is one of the basic mechanisms of the FIXON Partnership Program.

The aim of the discount system is to premium partners who develop the sale of FIXON products, increase their availability for final customers and actively use their distribution channels to build the market.

The discount system is progressive. This means that the level of the Partner's discount increases with the increase in the Sales Result.

The system was designed to be as simple as possible operationally, transparent to the Partner and linked to the actual sales volume of FIXON products.

The discount is not treated exclusively as a reduction in the purchase price. It is a tool for developing cooperation to enable the Partner:

  • building an attractive offer for customers,
  • increasing trade margins,
  • developing the sale of FIXON products,
  • handling larger orders,
  • development of relations with permanent recipients,
  • gradual transition to higher levels of cooperation.

The discount system should support sales partners, but should not lead to destabilization of the distribution network or aggressive lowering of FIXON brand value.

Discounts are charged in accordance with the Agreement, Partner Programme, Catalog Pricelist, Sales Results and other Program Documents.

8.2. Basic assumptions of the discount scheme

The discount system is based on the Sales Result achieved by the Partner.

The Sales result shall be determined together for FIXON products covered by the Programme, taking into account the rules on warehouse products, custom products, returns, complaints and exemptions.

The discount is determined on the basis of the best Sales Result achieved by the Partner in up to twelve previous Settlement Periods.

This means that a temporary decrease in sales in one Settlement Period does not automatically result in a loss of the discount level if the Partner has previously achieved a higher score qualifying for a higher threshold.

The discount system serves to stabilise cooperation and encourages Partner to build long-term sales, instead of focusing solely on the result of one month.

The discount granted on the basis of the best Sales Result shall apply for the next Settlement Period, unless the Agreement, Pricing, Partner Card or other Program Document provides otherwise.

The discount applies to all FIXON products unless Pricelist, Product List, Program, Contract or other Program Document provides for the exclusion of a particular product, product group or additional costs.

Delivery costs shall not be discounted if they are listed in the Pricelist or included as a separate element of the price calculation.

If the product catalog price presented to the customer takes account of the delivery cost from the Supplier, the method of settling such cost results from the Price list, invoice or other Program Document.

8.3. Discount thresholds

The basic discount thresholds in the FIXON Partnership Programme are:

  • 5 to 20 pieces — 5%,
  • 21 to 100 pieces — 10%,
  • 101 to 500 pieces — 15%,
  • from 501 pieces and more — 20%.

Discount thresholds are established on the basis of the Sales Result.

The Sales result may cover both the number of warehouse items sold and the Sales Units calculated on the basis of the sale of the products on order.

A partner that has reached a specific discount threshold shall benefit from the discount assigned to that threshold in the next Settlement Period.

If the Partner has achieved a higher Sales Result in the past over a period of up to twelve previous Settlement Periods, the best result applies to the determination of the discount level.

The supplier may grant additional bonuses, bonuses or special conditions to the Partner if the Partner performs above average sales results, operates a particularly valuable sales channel, supports significant customers or meets other conditions specified in the Program or individual Program Documents.

Additional bonuses, bonuses or special terms are not granted automatically and require the Supplier's decision.

8.4. Sales result

Sales result is the basis for establishing the Partner discount level.

The Sales result shall mean the Partner's total result determined for the relevant Settlement Period, taking into account the rules set out in the Programme, the Agreements and Program Documents.

The Sales result is counted together for all FIXON products covered by the Programme, unless Pricelist, Product List, Partner Card or other Program Document provides a special rule for certain products.

The Sales Result shall include in particular:

  • sold pieces of warehouse products,
  • sold items from the starting warehouse,
  • sold pieces of products covered by the Storage States Support Programme,
  • Sales units calculated from the value of sale to order of products,
  • returns if the sale was originally made and reported,
  • complaints if the sale was originally made and subject to reporting.

The Sales Result shall not include in particular:

  • normal consumption of exposure products,
  • normal use of test tools,
  • products damaged by Partner's fault,
  • products lost from Partner's fault,
  • products destroyed by Partner's fault,
  • products cleared solely because of the responsibility of the Partner,
  • operating articles,
  • other products indicated by the Supplier as excluded from the Sales Result.

The Sales result does not cover delivery costs if the cost of delivery is listed as a separate element of the price or settlement.

If the cost of delivery is included in the catalogue price of the product available with delivery from the Supplier, the way in which it is excluded or taken into account when calculating the discount may result from the Pricelist or invoice.

The Sales result should reflect the real business activity of the Partner rather than events arising solely from loss, damage or settlement of liability for the product.

8.5. Sales result and warehouse products

Storage products are included in the Sales Result by number of items sold.

The warehouse product is a product located with Partner in the starting warehouse or covered by the Storage States Support Program.

The sale of the storage product should be shown in the monthly sales report.

For the purposes of the Sales Result, one sold piece of the warehouse product corresponds to one unit of the Sales Result, unless the Supplier indicates a different rule for certain products.

Storage products sold to end customers, contractors, wholesale customers or other buyers are included in the Sales Result if the sales have been properly reported.

Products released, lost or damaged without sale to the customer do not include the Sales Result, even if they are invoiced to the Partner due to liability for the product.

8.6. Sales result and custom products

The custom products include the Sales Result by converting the value of their sales into Sales Units.

The conversion mechanism of products to order is intended to allow for the inclusion in the discount system of sales of products that are not counted by a simple number of items in the standard warehouse of the Partner.

This applies in particular to products:

  • made exclusively on individual order,
  • accessible with a longer deadline,
  • available on an individual basis,
  • not available as standard in Partner magazine,
  • with a higher unit value,
  • introduced to the offer in the procurement model.

The value of sales of products on order shall be converted to Sales Units by dividing the total net value of sales of such products in a given Settlement Period by the current catalogue net price of the most expensive product covered by the Warehouse States Support Program of the Partner.

The result of the conversion shall be rounded down to the full integer.

Technical example:

  • if the total net sales value of the products on order is PLN 12,000,
  • and the net price of the most expensive product covered by the Warehouse Support Programme is PLN 3 000,
  • the conversion result is 4 Sales Units.

If the result of the conversion does not give a full Sales Unit, the incomplete part does not increase the Sales Result.

This mechanism aims to maintain the proportion between the sale of standard products and custom products.

If the Partner is just beginning to cooperate and has a small range of warehouse support, the sale of a more expensive product on order may have a stronger impact on the Sales Result.

If the Partner has an extensive warehouse range, the same mechanism reduces the risk of excessive distortion of the result Sales by single expensive order.

Products available immediately with delivery from the Supplier may be part of the Warehouse States Support Program and the catalogue offer.

8.8. Reimbursements and Complaints in Sales Result

Reimbursements and complaints shall be included in the Sales Result if the sales of the product were actually made and reported.

This principle is based on the assumption that the Partner has made sales activity, which should be visible in the history of cooperation.

The inclusion of return or complaint in the Sales Result does not automatically mean that the product will be invoiced or billed in the same way as the sales completed without return or complaint.

The financial consequences of returns and complaints shall be determined in accordance with the Agreement, the Complaint Procedure, Price list and billing documents.

  • destroyed products,
  • products damaged by Partner's fault,
  • products which have not been identified during inventory,
  • products cleared due to lack of cooperation in the reception of the warehouse,
  • products which have been issued or used wrongly.

Non-exposure products damaged, lost or destroyed by Partner may be accounted for without discount unless the Supplier decides otherwise.

Settlement of Partner's liability cannot be treated as a sale with a discount.

CHAPTER 9
FIXON STRATEGY PARTNER

9.1. Nature of the status of Strategic Partner

The FIXON Strategic Partner is the second level of cooperation under the FIXON Partnership Programme.

The status of the Strategic Partner may be granted to the Authorized Partner FIXON, whose mode of operation, sales results, market access, customer base, role in product development or importance for the distribution network justify granting specific conditions of cooperation.

The Strategic Partner is not only a partner with a high turnover. This status may result from different sources of strategic value for FIXON.

The strategic value of the Partner may result in particular from:

  • permanent sale of FIXON products,
  • customer service with an above-average volume of purchases,
  • the acquisition of a significant wholesale customer,
  • the development of a particular sales channel,
  • the development of sales in a particular region,
  • the sale of custom-made products,
  • participation in the development of a new product,
  • providing feedback to the implementation of the product,
  • the organisation of a specific exposure or promotion model,
  • creating the conditions for launching the FIXON Demonstration Centre.

The status of the Strategic Partner is individual and recognised.

The Partner shall not be entitled to a claim to grant, maintain, extend or change the status of a Strategic Partner unless a separate document expressly provides otherwise.

Granting Strategic Partner status requires a Supplier's decision and confirmation in the Partner's Charter, the Strategic Powers Charter or other Program Document.

The status of the Strategic Partner shall be granted to the Partner as an entity, not as a single facility, unless the Parties individually agree on a specific model of operation of the respective mandate.

9.2. Objective of Strategic Partner status

The goal of the status of Strategic Partner is to allow for the extension of cooperation with the Partner, whose activities bring or can bring overstandard value to FIXON, Partner and final customers.

The status of the Strategic Partner is intended to allow the granting of such a power which corresponds to the actual source of value created by the Partner.

If the Partner generates above average purchase volumes, the appropriate tool may be Additional Strategic Discount.

If there is a restriction of internal competition in a particular channel of sales that is profitable for both parties, Market Protection may be the appropriate tool.

If the Partner continuously achieves high sales results and further development of sales requires greater availability of products, the Extended Storage States Support Program may be the appropriate tool.

If the Partner has contributed to the creation of a new product, the relevant tool may be Product Exclusive or Premier.

The status of the Strategic Partner is therefore functional. It is not an honorary title, but a basis for granting a specific model of further cooperation.

The purpose of the status is to increase the cost-effectiveness of cooperation for both parties by matching the benefits to the real potential of the Partner.

9.3. Strategic Partner as a stage in the development of cooperation

The Strategic Partner is a natural step in developing cooperation after the Authorised Partner level.

As a rule, the Strategic Partner must have the status of Authorised FIXON Partner in advance.

The status of the Strategic Partner can be granted after a period of cooperation allowing to assess results, quality reporting, timely settlements, the use of exposures and the potential for further development.

However, the programme allows a situation in which a particular business event warrants considering granting strategic partner status regardless of a long history of cooperation.

Such an event may in particular be:

  • acquisition of a significant wholesale customer,
  • the conclusion of a contract for large repetitive contracts,
  • launching a significant distribution channel,
  • Partner participation in the development of a new product,
  • creating the conditions for the implementation of the premiere product,
  • carrying out a promotional action of significant importance to FIXON,
  • propose a model of the Demonstration Centre,
  • another factor creating a strategic value for the Programme.

The status of the Strategic Partner can therefore result from both the sustainable potential of cooperation and from a one-time event of significant business importance.

In any case, the decision to grant the status requires an assessment of the Supplier.

9.4. Basic conditions for maintaining status

The Strategic Partner should meet the basic conditions for proper cooperation for all Partners.

The condition for maintaining the status of Strategic Partner is in particular:

  • proper reporting of sales,
  • proper reporting of stock,
  • timely payment arrangements,
  • no significant and unexplained storage gaps,
  • compliance with FIXON brand protection rules,
  • compliance with the rules on the use of marketing material,
  • use of the allocated Strategic Powers in accordance with its objective,
  • cooperation with the Supplier or Mediator in the implementation of the Programme,
  • non-use of status in a way contrary to the interests of FIXON.

The Strategic Partner shall conduct the exposure on a general basis unless the nature of its Strategic Power requires specific exposure obligations.

Additional exposure requirements may apply in particular to a Partner benefiting from Product Exclusive, Premier Exclusive, Test Products, Special Products or participation in promotional action.

If the Partner does not meet the basic conditions of cooperation, The supplier may refuse to grant the status of Strategic Partner, limit its scope, suspend the Strategic Entitlement or revoke the status.

9.5. Scenarios for granting Strategic Partner status

The status of the Strategic Partner may be granted in different scenarios, depending on the value created by the Partner.

The programme distinguishes in particular four main scenarios:

  • the discount scenario,
  • market scenario,
  • storage scenario,
  • product or premiere scenario.

The catalog of scenarios is not closed. The supplier may also grant the status of Strategic Partner in other cases if he considers that this is cost-effective and justified for both parties.

In the future, the Programme can be expanded with additional strategic scenarios, without the need to change the overall logic of the Programme.

9.6. Discount scenario

The discount scenario concerns the Partner, who built a database of regular customers generating above average purchasing volumes.

This scenario may also apply to a Partner who operates a significant wholesale customer, a group of professional customers, repeatable customers or a sales channel requiring more price flexibility.

In this case, the Partner may receive an Additional Strategic Discount.

The aim of the Additional Strategic Rabat is to enable the Partner to:

  • building your own model of customer loyalty support,

  • handling larger orders,
  • increase competitiveness in contacts with wholesale customers,
  • maintaining a long-term relationship with clients with high potential,
  • increase the margin while maintaining an attractive offer to the recipient,
  • larger-scale sales shares.

The discount scenario should be applied when additional price flexibility can translate into a real increase in sales, maintenance of relevant customers or development of the sales channel.

The award of the Additional Strategic Rabat requires the definition of its scope in the Charter of Strategic Power or other Program Document.

9.7. Market scenario

The market scenario relates to a situation where a specific sales channel, area, customer group, design or segment of the market requires the arrangement of cooperation rules.

In some sales channels, the existence of a number of competitors offering the same products may distort cooperation, reduce its profitability or hinder market development.

If granting protection to a particular market, channel or client brings benefits to both parties, the Partner may obtain Market Protection as Strategic Entitlement.

Market protection may concern in particular:

  • territory,
  • industry,
  • sales channel,
  • customer groups,
  • project,
  • investments,
  • networks of establishments,
  • a specific client or group of clients,
  • another agreed scope.

Market protection does not cover a product group as access to certain products, variants or prime minister is subject to Product Exclusive or Premiere.

Market protection may in particular mean:

  • no active acquisition of competing partners in the protected range,
  • preferential referral to the Partner,
  • limiting competitive activities in a given channel,
  • agreeing on the terms of service of a specific client or investment,
  • the protection of the sales model developed by the Partner.

Market protection does not automatically mean full exclusivity.

Full exclusivity may only be granted on the basis of a separate decision of the Supplier and must be clearly specified in the Charter of Strategic Power or another Programme Document.

9.8. Storage scenario

The storage scenario concerns a Partner who generates fixed, above average sales results or whose sales model justifies a higher level of product availability.

If a Partner regularly sells certain products in quantities justifying their larger handling, he may obtain an Extended Warehouse States Support Program.

The main objective of this scenario is to optimise logistics costs, reduce customer service times and increase the availability of products with proven rotation.

The Extended Warehouse States Support Program may consist in particular of increasing the level of state replenishment from 40% to 80% of the highest sales result of a given item from up to twelve previous Settlement Periods.

The authorisation may concern:

  • all products,
  • parts of products,
  • selected product groups,
  • products available immediately with delivery from the Supplier,
  • custom products,
  • special products,
  • products of significant importance to the Partner's sales channel.

Regular sale of a custom-made product may justify provision of storage support if this is cost-effective for both parties.

The increased storage limits should be indicated in the Product List for Storage Support.

The storage supplements of the Partner using this power shall be carried out on a general basis, unless the individual document expressly provides for another solution.

9.9. Product or premiere scenario

The product or premiere scenario concerns a Partner whose actions have led to the creation of a new product, a product variant or a significant direction in the development of FIXON offer.

The activity itself in collecting feedback from customers is not sufficient to grant Product Exclusive or Premiere.

The basis for granting such a right is that a product or an important product variant has been created on the basis of information, observation, needs or proposals provided by the Partner.

If the idea of a product comes from a Partner or Partner played an important role in its creation, the implementation of the product should generally start with that Partner.

The Partner may be granted Prime Exclusive for the distribution of the newly introduced product for a period of at least 3 months after the product has been placed on the market.

The Prime Minister may include in particular:

  • priority of receiving test copies,
  • possibility of placing the product in selected sales channels of the Partner,
  • the possibility of organising promotional actions,
  • the possibility to apply for direct handling of selected trading points with a new product,
  • priority of commercial communication related to the product,
  • participation in the collection of market opinion after product implementation.

The placement of selected shopping points of the new product as part of the promotional action is not the default and requires agreement with the Supplier.

Failure to provide the required feedback during the test or launch period may result in loss of Product Exclusive or Premiere, without automatic loss of Strategic Partner status.

In this case, the Strategic Partner may apply for a different Strategic Power if the Supplier considers that the continuation of the strategic status is justified.

CHAPTER 10
STRATEGIC POWERS OF A STRATEGIC PARTNER

10.1. Nature of Strategic Entitlements

Strategic powers are special benefits granted to the Strategic Partner under the FIXON Partnership Programme.

Strategic Entitlement is a tool for matching cooperation with the specific value created by the Partner.

Strategic Entitlement is not an automatic award for the very fact of participation in the Programme, but a individually awarded mechanism for the development of cooperation.

Strategic powers may serve in particular:

  • increasing sales,
  • building loyalty of the Partner's customers,
  • protecting a viable sales channel,
  • optimization of logistics costs,
  • increasing product availability,
  • implementation of special products,
  • introduction of premiere products,
  • development of new products on the basis of market feedback,
  • building the partner's competitive advantage,
  • preparing the Partner to become the FIXON Demonstration Centre.

Strategic power should be granted in such a way as to strengthen a cooperation model which is cost-effective for both parties.

Just describing Strategic Powers in the Program does not mean that the Partner acquires the right to it.

Granting Strategic Power requires a decision of the Supplier and confirmation in the Charter of Strategic Powers, Partner Charter, Contract or other Program Document.

10.2. Catalogue of Strategic Entitlements

The FIXON Partnership Programme provides for the following core Strategic Rights:

  • Additional Strategic Discount,
  • Market protection,
  • Extended Warehouse States Support Program,
  • Product exclusive or Premier exclusive.

The catalogue of Strategic Rights can be expanded in the future if the development of the Programme, products, distribution network or partners' needs warrant new benefits.

The development of the catalogue of Strategic Rights can take place either by updating the Partnership Program, Program Documents or by adopting a separate document describing the new entitlement.

Granting a new type of power to the Partner requires its individual confirmation.

  • discount,
  • market
  • storage,
  • Product

  • Exposure
  • mixed.

The Strategic Partner shall in principle receive one leading Strategic Authority.

Leading Strategic Entitlement means the main benefit, which best corresponds to the source of value created by the Partner.

If the Partner creates value primarily through high purchase volumes and a permanent customer base, the leading right may be Additional Strategic Discount.

If the Partner creates value through the development of a particular sales channel, territory, customer, industry or investment, Market Protection may be the leading right.

If the Partner creates value through constant sales requiring higher availability of products, the leading permission may be the Extended Warehouse States Support Program.

If the Partner creates value by participating in the creation of a new product, the leading right may be Product Exclusive or Premier.

The leading Strategic Authority has the widest range of benefits granted to the Partner.

The partner may use additional elements of other powers if they are granted, but their scope may be limited to the leading power.

For example, a Partner whose leading right is the Extended Warehouse States Support Program may receive limited channel protection if justified, but this does not automatically mean full Market Protection.

The scope of the leading and possible additional powers should be specified in the Charter of Strategic Powers or other Programme Document.

The Supplier in principle proposes to the Strategic Partner such Strategic Entitlement, which best suits the nature of the cooperation and the source of the value created by the Partner.

The Supplier's proposal does not preclude the Partner from choosing another power.

If the Partner considers that other Strategic Entitlement is better suited to his model of action, he may make such a proposal to the Supplier or Mediator.

The supplier shall consider the Partner's proposal taking into account the interest of FIXON brand, the cost-effectiveness of cooperation, the impact on the distribution network and the feasibility of the proposed model.

The final scope of Strategic Powers is agreed individually.

10.6. Additional Strategic Discount

10.6.1. Objective of the Additional Strategic Rabat

An Additional Strategic Discount is a Discount Strategic Entitlement.

The aim of the Additional Strategic Discount is to enable the Partner to increase commercial flexibility in situations where the Partner develops sales of over-standard importance to the Programme.

Additional Strategic Discount may serve in particular:

  • the construction of the Partner's own loyalty programme,
  • rewarding the regular recipients of the Partner,
  • wholesale customer service,
  • handling large investment orders,
  • maintaining customers with high potential,
  • construction of a repetitive volume of purchases,
  • enhancing the competitiveness of the Partner in large negotiations,
  • support for larger-scale sales shares,
  • securing the Partner margin while maintaining the attractiveness of the offer,
  • building long-term cooperation with professional clients.

Additional Strategic Discount may be particularly justified when a Partner has a database of regular customers whose loyalty can be strengthened by individual sales conditions.

This discount may also be justified when the Partner regularly serves customers with large volumes for which the basic discount system does not provide sufficient commercial flexibility.

The additional Strategic Discount should support the development of sales rather than lead to aggressive price dumping or network destabilisation.

10.6.2. Additional Strategic Rabat

Additional Strategic Discount may be up to 5% inclusive.

The exact level of discount is determined individually by the Supplier.

The level of the discount should be indicated in the Charter of Strategic Powers, Partner Charter or other Program Document.

The award of the maximum level of the Additional Strategic Rabat is not automatic.

The supplier may grant a lower level of discount if this is justified by the scale of cooperation, the nature of the customers, the sales structure, the cost-effectiveness of the model or the stage of development of the Partner.

Additional Strategic Discount may be applied to the entire FIXON offer or to selected products, product groups, customers, sales channels or projects, if specified in the award document.

10.6.3. Application of the Additional Strategic Discount by the Partner

Partner can use Additional Strategic Discount to build its own trade policy towards its customers.

In particular, the Partner may use an additional discount to:

  • the creation of tenders for permanent recipients,
  • creating conditions for wholesale customers,
  • the remuneration of returnees,
  • strengthening the loyalty of executive teams,
  • supporting seasonal actions,
  • handling larger orders,
  • preparation of investment offers,
  • increase the partner's own margin.

The Partner should use the Additional Strategic Discount in accordance with FIXON brand protection rules.

The additional Strategic Discount should not be used for activities which could permanently reduce the perceived value of FIXON products or lead to aggressive price dumping.

If the Partner plans to use Additional Strategic Discount in a large-scale price campaign, he should agree such action with the Supplier or Mediator.

10.6.4. Limiting or withdrawing the Additional Strategic Rabat

Additional Strategic Discount may be reduced, suspended or withdrawn in the event of violations on the part of the Partner.

The restriction or withdrawal may be based in particular on:

  • breach of the Agreement,
  • violation of the Programme,
  • no reporting,
  • late payment,
  • the use of the discount contrary to its purpose,
  • aggressive price dumping,
  • actions affecting the image of FIXON,
  • granting a discount in a destabilizing way to the network,
  • lack of cooperation in clearing accounts,
  • breach of commercial communications rules.

The decline in sales does not result in the automatic loss of the Additional Strategic Rabat.

However, a sustained decrease in sales may form the basis for reviewing the appropriateness of continuing to maintain the discount if the loss of volume affects the profitability of cooperation.

Before changing or withdrawing the discount The supplier may propose to the Partner to change the strategic model or to choose another Strategic Power if further strategic cooperation is justified.

10.7. Market protection

10.7.1. Objective of Market Protection

Market protection is a market-based strategic right.

The objective of Market Protection is to organise cooperation in a specific area, channel, segment, project or customer relationship if the restriction of internal competition benefits both parties.

Market protection may be granted where the existence of many competitors in the same range could:

  • disrupt cooperation,
  • weaken the cost-effectiveness of partner activities,
  • lead to excessive price competition,
  • hinder the development of the market,
  • reduce the quality of customer service,
  • double sales activities,
  • hinder the construction of relationships with a large client,
  • limit the effectiveness of promotional or implementation actions.

Market protection is to support a Partner who actively develops a specific area of cooperation and whose activities require the stability of the sales channel.

Market protection is not granted automatically and requires individual decision of the Supplier.

10.7.2. Scope of market protection

Market protection may include in particular:

  • defined territory,
  • specific industry,
  • a specific sales channel,
  • a specific group of customers,
  • a specific project,
  • the specific investment,
  • a network of partner facilities,
  • a specific customer,
  • a group of related customers,
  • other agreed market coverage.

Market protection does not apply to the product group as such. Access to certain products, variants or prime minister is regulated by Product Exclusive or Premiere.

The scope of Market Protection must be described in the Charter of Strategic Powers or other Programme Document.

The instrument granting market protection should specify in particular:

  • as regards protection,
  • since it applies,
  • the period of validity,
  • whether it involves the lack of active acquisition of competing partners,
  • whether it includes preferential questioning,
  • whether it includes full exclusivity,
  • what are the obligations of the Protected Partner.

10.7.3. Forms of market protection

Market protection may vary in intensity.

Basic Market Protection may mean that the Supplier will not actively acquire competing partners in the protected range.

Extended Market Protection may also mean preferential directing queries, lead or customers to a Partner.

Full exclusivity can only be granted if clearly indicated in the Charter of Strategic Powers.

Market protection may also include an agreement on customer, project or investment service rules.

For customers who apply directly to FIXON, The supplier may:

  • service the customer directly,
  • redirect the client to a Partner with Market Protection,
  • service the customer together with the Partner,
  • establish an individual service model,
  • use another model justified by the interest of the client and FIXON brand.

The Supplier shall decide on the manner of service of customers directly to FIXON, taking into account the scope of the protection granted to the Partner.

10.7.4. Obligations of the Market Protection Partner

A market-based partner should actively develop a protected scope of cooperation.

In particular, the Partner should:

  • maintain a protected range of sales activities,
  • handle customers with due care,
  • inform the Supplier or the Mediator of market developments,
  • report sales in accordance with the rules of the Programme,
  • not use protection only to block the market,
  • take care of the image of FIXON brand,
  • avoid activities that may destabilise relations with customers or other partners.

Market protection should not lead to the passiveness of the Partner.

If the Partner does not develop the protected scope, The supplier may review the appropriateness of maintaining the protection.

10.7.5. Limitation or withdrawal of market protection

Market protection may be restricted, suspended or revoked in the case of:

  • lack of partner activity in the protected range,
  • not exploiting market potential,
  • violations of the Programme rules,
  • violations of the principles of brand protection,
  • loss of cost-effectiveness of protection for one or both parties,
  • creating a conflict with the interests of the client,
  • changes in market conditions,
  • changes in the development strategy of the FIXON network,
  • violations of the obligations set out in the Charter of Strategic Powers.

The limitation of market protection may consist in particular of:

  • reducing the territorial scope,
  • limiting the range of customers,
  • excluding a particular project,
  • changing the form of protection,
  • the end of preferential guidance,
  • changing Market Protection to other Strategic Entitlement.

10.8. Extended Warehouse States Support Program

10.8.1. Objective of the Extended Storage States Support Program

The Extended Warehouse States Support Program is a storage strategic power.

The purpose of this Power is to increase the availability of products with the Partner, which achieves stable, above average sales results or regularly sells products requiring a specific storage model.

This authorisation is intended to optimise logistics costs, shorten customer service time and increase sales efficiency.

The Extended Warehouse States Support Program may be particularly justified when:

  • The partner regularly achieves high sales results,
  • The partner often launches emergency calls,
  • the basic level of 40% is not sufficient to ensure the liquidity of sales,
  • Partner serves wholesale customers,
  • Partner conducts sales in high-dynamic seasons,
  • The Partner regularly sells products available immediately with delivery from the Supplier,
  • Partner regularly sells custom-made products,
  • a larger stock reduces logistics costs for both parties.

10.8.2. Scope of the Extended Programme

The main benefit of the Extended Warehouse States Support Program is the possibility to increase the level of state replenishment from 40% to 80% of the best sales result.

The increase may concern:

  • all products,
  • parts of products,
  • the selected product group,
  • products available immediately with delivery from the Supplier,
  • custom products,
  • special products,
  • products relevant to the specific sales channel of the Partner.

The increased limits should be included in the Product List for Storage Support.

The status of the Strategic Partner does not mean automatically increasing the limits of all products.

The scope of the increase depends on the Strategic Rights Charter and the current List of Products covered by Storage Support.

10.8.3. Custom products in the Extended Program

Regular sale of custom-made products may justify placing them under storage support.

This applies especially to products which are not normally stored with Authorized Partners, but their sales to the Partner confirm the existence of constant demand.

Covering the product on order with warehouse support may allow the Partner to shorten customer service time and increase the attractiveness of the offer.

Such inclusion requires individual decision of the Supplier.

The on-order product covered by storage support should be indicated in the List of Products covered by storage support.

10.8.4. Supplements to the Extended Program

A partner benefiting from the Extended Warehouse Support Scheme shall be subject to general supplement rules unless otherwise provided for in the individual document.

FIXON does not, in principle, provide for the separation of the categories of warehouse supplies according to the status of the Partner.

This means that simply having an Extended Storage States Support Program does not automatically mean a separate logistics path or priority delivery.

Partner's benefit is primarily due to higher levels of states rather than different delivery frequencies.

Complementary deliveries are carried out in accordance with the rules of the Warehouse States Support Program.

10.8.5. Limitation of Extended Program

The Extended Warehouse States Support Program may be restricted, suspended or withdrawn if the maintenance of elevated states loses justification.

The restriction may be based in particular on:

  • decrease in product turnover,
  • no reporting,
  • significant storage gaps,
  • late payment,
  • lack of cooperation in inventory,
  • use of products not in accordance with the Programme,
  • changes in product availability,
  • the revision of the FIXON storage strategy,
  • loss of profitability in maintaining elevated states.

The restriction may apply to all products or only parts of products subject to the increased limit.

10.9. Product or Premier Exclusive

10.9.1. Objective of Product Exclusiveness or Prime Minister

Product exclusive or Premiere exclusive is a product-based strategic right.

The purpose of this right is to pay a Partner who has actually contributed to the creation of a new product, product variant or significant FIXON product solution.

The basis for granting this right is not the collection of feedback itself, but the situation where a product or a relevant product variant is produced on the basis of the information provided.

Product exclusive or Premiere is intended to encourage Partners to actively share knowledge from the market, observe performers, customer needs and propose new solutions.

At the same time, this mandate is intended to give the Partner priority to the use of the effect of his cooperation with FIXON.

10.9.2. Moment of creation of permission

If the idea of a product comes from a Partner or Partner has played an important role in the development of a product, the implementation of the product starts in principle with that Partner.

The test or premiere product first goes to the Partner initiating the product.

Only in further stages can the product be made available to other participants of the Programme.

Granting Product Exclusive or Premiere requires a decision of the Supplier and confirmation in the Charter of Strategic Powers or other Program Document.

The supplier may refuse to grant exclusivity if the product was created independently of the Partner, was previously developed by FIXON or the Partner's information had no significant impact on its development.

10.9.3. The Prime Minister’s exclusive period

The Prime Minister shall be exclusive for at least 3 months after the product has been placed on the market.

The supplier may set a longer exclusivity period for the product concerned.

The supplier may also set a different product implementation cycle where the nature of the product, seasonality, production, logistics or market strategy so requires.

The exclusivity period should be specified in the Charter of Strategic Powers or other Programme Document.

After the end of the exclusivity period, the product may be made available to subsequent participants in the Programme in accordance with the adopted implementation path.

10.9.4. Order of implementation of the product

In principle, the product developed with the Partner shall be implemented in the following order:

  • Partner initiating the product,
  • FIXON Demonstration Center,
  • Strategic Partners with Product Exclusiveness,
  • other Partners.

Each stage of implementation should last at least 3 months, unless the Supplier determines a different cycle for a given product.

The supplier may change the order of implementation, if the nature of the product so requires, the safety of the tests, production availability, seasonality or sales strategy.

The originator partner may receive test copies for selected sales channels or facilities.

The partner may apply for a new product to directly place trading points in order to organise a promotional action, but such a promotion is not a default and requires agreement with the Supplier.

10.9.5. Obligation to provide feedback

A partner using Product Exclusive or Premier is obliged to provide feedback on the product.

Feedback may relate in particular to:

  • customer reactions,
  • functionality of the product,
  • quality of performance,
  • expected changes,
  • operational problems,
  • the need for additional options,
  • purchasing interest,
  • potential uses,
  • promotional material,
  • the price and method of communication of the product.

  • date of sale,
  • the date of refund,
  • the reason for the refund, if known,
  • the method of settlement of the refund.

In the field of complaint, the report shall indicate:

  • product name,
  • number of pieces,
  • the date of notification,
  • the basic description of the notification,
  • the status of the complaint, if known,
  • whether the product has been returned, replaced, repaired or remains with the customer.

For damaged or lost products, the report shall indicate:

  • product name,
  • number of pieces,
  • description of the event,
  • date of disclosure,
  • whether the event concerns a storage, display, demonstration or testing product.

The partner should report the data reliably and in a way that allows verification.

If the Partner does not have all the data at the time of submitting the report, he should indicate the available data and inform him of the need to complete the report.

12.4. Alert

An alert may be filed by the Partner at any time of the Settlement Period if at least one item covered by the Warehouse Support Program reaches the Alarm Threshold.

An alert does not replace a monthly report.

An emergency notification should contain the current storage status of all items covered by the Storage States Support Program.

The requirement to indicate the status of all items results from the fact that a supplement after an emergency notification may cover the entire warehouse, not only the product that reached the Alarm Threshold.

An alert shall include in particular:

  • Partner data,
  • the date of notification,
  • indication of the product which has reached the Alarm Threshold,
  • current status of all products supported,
  • indication of the products sold since the last report,
  • indication of returned products,
  • indication of products damaged or lost,
  • information on the products advertised,
  • information on products blocked or not released,
  • other data required by the Supplier or Mediator.

If the emergency call is incomplete, The supplier or intermediary may call on the Partner to supplement it before completing the complementary delivery.

The supplier may suspend the execution of the complementary supply until complete data is received.

An emergency notification should be submitted in a form agreed with the Supplier or Mediator.

12.5. No monthly report

The lack of a monthly report prevents the proper functioning of the Programme.

In the absence of a monthly report:

  • it is impossible to charge a discount,
  • proper invoicing is impossible,
  • the completion of the warehouse is suspended,
  • the update of stock levels is suspended,
  • the assessment of the Sales Result is suspended,
  • the assessment of the grounds for granting benefits is suspended.

In the absence of a monthly report, the Supplier or Mediator calls on the Partner to submit a report.

Until a proper report is submitted, the Supplier may suspend the additional deliveries, the transfer of additional products, the processing of increases in stock and other activities requiring sales data.

If the Partner fails to report within 30 days of the request, The supplier may terminate the Agreement in accordance with its provisions.

If the Agreement is terminated due to lack of reporting, the Supplier may collect the warehouse at the Partner's expense and settle the actual final stock.

If the Partner does not cooperate on the reception of the warehouse, The supplier may invoice the entire warehouse transferred without applying a discount.

Failure to report may also form the basis for withdrawing the status of Strategic Partner, limiting Strategic Entitlements or refusing to grant further benefits.

12.6. Invoices and accounts

The invoice shall be issued on the basis of a monthly report, sales declarations, storage documents or other documents proving the sale or event to be settled.

The invoice may include in particular:

  • products sold by the Partner,
  • products sold from the starting warehouse,
  • products sold from the Warehouse States Support Program,
  • products available immediately with delivery from the Supplier,
  • custom products,
  • products lost,
  • damaged products,
  • products destroyed,
  • products accounted for by Partner,
  • delivery costs, if billed,
  • other items resulting from the Agreement or Program Documents.

Products sold to customers are invoiced taking into account the Partner's discount in accordance with Sales Results and Pricelist.

Exposure, demonstration or test products lost, destroyed or damaged by Partner's fault may be invoiced with a 50% discount on the catalogue price, unless Program Documents provide otherwise.

Non-exposure products lost, damaged or damaged by Partner's fault may be invoiced without discount unless the Supplier decides otherwise.

The settlement of products lost, destroyed or damaged by Partner's fault is not treated as Sales Result and does not increase the Partner's discount level.

The deadline for payment of the invoice is 7 days unless the Agreement, the Partner Card or other Program Document provides for another deadline.

Electronic invoices are a standard form of billing documentation.

After implementation of the existing KSeF system, invoices may be issued and delivered in accordance with the rules of that system.

The billing of the cost of delivery of the products on order may be recorded on the invoice if this results from Pricelist, sales model or rules of the product concerned.

12.7. Accounting corrections

Clearances may be adjusted if, after the invoice has been issued, the circumstances affecting its regularity become apparent.

The amendment may relate in particular to:

  • misreported sales,
  • omitted sale,
  • incorrectly indicated product,
  • an incorrect price,
  • an incorrect discount,
  • mistaking the cost of delivery,
  • reimbursement,
  • complaints,
  • storage gaps,
  • damaged or lost product,
  • accounting error,
  • misclassification of the product as being included or not included in the Sales Result.

The correction may be made by an adjustment invoice, compensation, note, correction of the report or other document relevant to the event.

The Partner should immediately inform the Supplier or the Mediator of the circumstances which may lead to the need for correction.

Repeated reporting errors may affect the assessment of the Partner and the possibility of using the benefits of the Program.

12.8. Complaints — General Principles

Complaints of FIXON products are handled by the Supplier or manufacturer in accordance with the FIXON Complaint Procedure.

The Partner may accept a complaint from the Customer and forward it to the Supplier.

The Customer may also submit a complaint directly to the Supplier if such a notification is available.

The complaint risk of the product shall be borne by the manufacturer if the complaint is justified and the defect is not due to Partner's fault.

The Partner shall not bear the costs of the accepted complaint if the defect of the product is not due to his fault, improper storage, misrelease, mechanical damage on the Partner's side or any other act or omission of the Partner.

Complaints should be submitted and documented in such a way as to assess the appropriateness of the notification.

Detailed rules for the acceptance, documentation, processing and settlement of complaints shall be laid down in a separate Complaint Procedure.

12.9. Complaint

Complaints should contain data enabling product identification and assessment of the application.

The notification may include in particular:

  • product name,
  • date of sale,
  • customer data or order number, if available,
  • description of the defect reported,
  • the circumstances of the failure,
  • photographs of the product,
  • a video showing the problem, if needed,
  • the sales document,
  • information on whether the product is at the customer, Partner or has been returned,
  • the customer's expectation if it has been notified.

The supplier may request additional information or documents if they are needed to handle the complaint.

The Partner should cooperate with the Supplier in explaining the complaint.

The lack of data necessary for the handling of the complaint may prolong its processing time.

12.10. Procedures for handling complaints

After considering the complaint, the Supplier may in particular:

  • accept the complaint,
  • refuse to accept the complaint,
  • request additional information,
  • propose repair of the product,
  • propose the exchange of products,
  • propose the provision of the missing element,
  • propose another solution justified by the nature of the case.

If the complaint is accepted, the manner in which it is implemented shall be determined in accordance with the Complaint Procedure and the rules applicable to the sales concerned.

If the defect is due to Partner's fault, improper storage, mechanical damage or incorrect use of the product by Partner, The supplier may refuse to cover the costs of the complaint.

Complaints should be included in the monthly report in order to allow proper settlement of stock stocks and Sales Result.

A recognized complaint does not burden the Partner unless the defect is due to his fault.

12.11. Refunds

The product returns shall be recorded in sales and settlement reports in accordance with the rules of the Programme, the Agreement and the relevant Programme Documents.

The refund of the product should be indicated in the monthly report.

The phrase shall indicate:

  • the product,
  • number of pieces,
  • date of sale,
  • date of refund,
  • the state of the product,
  • the reason for reimbursement, if known,
  • the method of further settlement.

Reimbursements shall be taken into account in the Sales Result if the sales of the product were actually made and reported.

The financial consequences of reimbursement may be accounted for separately in accordance with accounting documents and rules agreed by the Parties.

The returned product in a condition allowing for further sale may be reintroduced into the Partner's warehouse.

Returned product damaged or incomplete shall be assessed in accordance with the rules on complaint, liability of the Partner or return procedure.

12.12. Products damaged, lost and destroyed

The partner should immediately report damaged, lost or destroyed products.

The notification shall contain a description of the incident and an indication of whether the product was:

  • storage product,
  • the product from the starting warehouse,
  • the exposure product,
  • a test tool,
  • a demonstration product,
  • Test or Premier Product.

Products lost, damaged or damaged by Partner's fault shall be accounted for in accordance with the Program and the Agreement.

Exposure, demonstration or testing products accounted for by the Partner may be accounted for with a 50% discount on the catalogue price, unless otherwise specified.

Non-exposure products accounted for by Partner can be cleared without discount.

The settlement of such products is not the result of the sale.

Normal consumption of exposure or test products resulting from their demonstration function is not considered as a damage on the part of the Partner.

12.13. Program Documents

Program documents are complementary documents to the FIXON Partnership Programme, specifying detailed cooperation rules, price lists, procedures, product lists, status cards and other operational elements.

The list of Program Documents is open.

The programming documents shall include in particular:

  • FIXON Partner Programme,

  • Partner card,

  • The Strategic Powers Charter,

  • Protocol for the transmission of products,
  • Inventory Protocol,
  • other documents published or transmitted by the Supplier.

Program documents may be expanded in the future without the need to change the entire Programme, unless this violates the Partnership Agreement.

If the Programme Document defines the individual conditions of the Partner, it should be submitted to the Partner in a form that allows it to be recorded.

Documents submitted to the Partner under the FIXON Partner Programme, in particular the Partner Card, Catalog Pricelist, List of Products Covered by Storage Support, Product Status, Procedures, Forms, Instructions, Information Cards, Updates of Cooperation Principles and Other Documents related to the current implementation of the Program, may be transmitted to the Partner in document form, in particular by email, CRM system, B2B system, communicator, message from the Cooperation Agent or otherwise enabling the Partner to read the content of the document. The document submitted to the Partner in this way does not require personal signature if the Partner does not object to its content within 7 days of its delivery. The absence of reservations within this deadline means the adoption of a document for information and use in the framework of current cooperation in the FIXON Partnership Programme. Documents transmitted under the Programme may also bear an electronic signature, a digital signature, a trusted signature, a qualified electronic signature or another form of electronic confirmation accepted by the Parties. If a Partner raises objections to any document within 7 days of its delivery, that document shall not be adopted in the absence of reservations in the scope of the notification and the Parties shall clarify its content, remove any discrepancies or prepare the final agreed version of the document. The final version of the document to which reservations have been made shall be approved by both Parties in the form of personal signatures or electronic signatures accepted by the Parties, unless otherwise agreed by the Parties in document correspondence. Pending approval of the final version of such a document, the existing version of the document or the existing rules shall apply unless the Parties agree on a provisional solution in documentary form. A statement of objections to one document shall not suspend the validity of other documents submitted under the FIXON Partnership Programme, unless the scope of the objections prevents their proper application.

The Partner Card may indicate in particular:

The Partner Card can be updated with a change in the status of Partner, discount, scope of cooperation or other conditions.

The Partner Charter does not need to repeat all the rules of the Programme, but defines individual cooperation parameters.

The Strategic Powers Charter defines the individual scope of Strategic Powers granted to the Strategic Partner.

The Strategic Powers Charter may cover in particular:

The Charter should indicate the scope of the mandate in a way that avoids doubts about what the Partner can expect and what obligations it must perform.

If the Strategic Power is not confirmed in the Charter or other document, A partner cannot rely on his admission.

12.17. Partner's Brands Policy and Own Materials

The partner can create their own advertising, promotional material, photos, videos, graphics, product testing and campaigns for FIXON products, subject to the supplier's approval.

Partner's own materials should be in line with FIXON brand image, current product information and fair commercial communication principles.

The Partner may publish its own FIXON product tests if their content has been accepted by the Supplier or if The supplier agreed to this form of publication.

The Partner can compare FIXON products with competitive products, but such comparisons require the Supplier's consent.

Comparisons should be fair, justified and should not violate the good name of the FIXON brand or third parties.

Once accepted, the advertising campaign may be repeated without the need to obtain further acceptance, provided it is conducted in the same content, in the same communication sense and without changes that may affect the reception of the message.

Changing content, graphics, passwords, prices, technical parameters, comparisons or the scope of the campaign may require re-acceptance.

The Partner may use the FIXON logo only in accordance with the materials and guidelines provided by the Supplier.

Detailed rules for brand communication, creation of own materials and obtaining acceptance are set out in FIXON Brand Policy.

The supplier may update the FIXON Partner Programme and Program Documents.

Updates may result in particular from:

Changes to the Programme and Program Documents shall enter into force from the date indicated by the Supplier, but not earlier than the next Settlement Period.

The beneficial or technical changes shall also enter into force from the date indicated by the Supplier, but not earlier than the next Settlement Period, unless the individual agreement with the Partner provides otherwise.

The current version of the Partnership Program can be published on the FIXON website.

The Partner should be informed of changes to the Program or Program Documents by the Supplier or Mediator.

Information on changes may be communicated in particular:

  • by email,
  • through the Mediator,
  • through the CRM system,
  • via the B2B system,
  • by publication on the website,
  • in another document form.

The program may be updated, but updates should not impair the individual conditions of the Partners resulting from existing Agreements, Partner Cards or other individual documents.

The individual terms of the Partner remain binding insofar as they are confirmed in the Agreement, the Partner Charter, the Charter of Strategic Powers, the Charter of the Demonstration Centre or other individual document.

12.19. Priority of Documents

In the event of a discrepancy between the Partnership Programme and the Partnership Agreement, the provisions of the Partnership Agreement shall prevail.

In the event of a discrepancy between the Programme and the Partner's individual document, priority shall be given to the individual document insofar as it sets out specific terms of cooperation and does not violate the Agreement.

In the event of a discrepancy between the General Program Document and the Partner Card, the Strategic Permission Card or the Demonstration Centre Card, priority shall be given to the individual document.

In case of interpretation doubts, documents should be interpreted in accordance with the objective of the Programme, the interest of FIXON brand, the stability of cooperation and the principle of transparency towards the Partner.

12.20. Final provisions of the Programme

The FIXON Partnership Programme lays down general principles of cooperation in the FIXON Partnership Network.

The Programme is an annex to the Partnership Agreement and is accepted by the Partner when signing the Agreement.

The program can be published on the FIXON website to allow partners and potential partners to get acquainted with the cooperation model.

Public provision of the Program does not mean that each Partner is entitled to all described statuses, rebates, allowances, limits and benefits.

The individual terms of the Partner result from the Agreement, Partner Charter, Strategic Rights Charter, Demonstration Centre Charter, List of Products Covered by Storage Support, Pricelist and other Program Documents.

The program should be used in a way that supports the development of a professional distribution network, FIXON brand protection, product availability, customer service quality and cost-effectiveness of cooperation for both parties.

In matters not regulated in the Programme, the Partnership Agreement, the relevant Programme Documents and the individual arrangements of the Parties shall apply.

The FIXON Partnership Programme shall enter into force either on the date indicated by the Supplier or on the date of its adoption as an annex to the Partnership Agreement.
